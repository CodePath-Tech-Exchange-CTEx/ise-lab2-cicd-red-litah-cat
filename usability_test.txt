
workout log feature:
Task Difficulty

The participant found the task fairly easy overall, especially once they understood the layout. However, the initial experience was slightly confusing, which slowed them down at the start.

“Once I figured it out, it was easy, but at first I didn’t know where to go.”

Confusions & Why They Happened


1. Navigation Tabs Were Confusing

Confusion:
The participant struggled to choose between “Goals,” “Workout Plans,” and “Log Workouts.”

Why:
These tabs felt similar and lacked descriptions or visual differences.

“I don’t know which one shows my workouts for today.”

2. Logging vs Completing Workouts

Confusion:
The participant thought checking a box meant logging a workout.

Why:
The checkbox was unclear and not labeled, making it look like the main action.

“I thought this would log it, but I guess it doesn’t?”

3. Hidden Actions (Start Workout / Share)

Confusion:
The participant didn’t notice key actions like “Stare with friend” or sharing to the community.

Why:
These actions were hidden behind interactions (expand cards or “•••” menu).

“I didn’t even see a stare icon.”
“I wouldn’t think to click the three dots for that.”

4. Back Navigation Unclear

Confusion:
The participant didn’t know where the back arrow would take them.

Why:
There was no context or label explaining the navigation.

“I don’t know where this goes back to.”

Results: Issues, Hypotheses, and Remedies
Issue 1: Unclear Navigation Tabs
Hypothesis: Tabs are too similar and lack visual guidance
Remedy: Add short descriptions and icons; use a bottom navigation bar
Issue 2: Confusing Checkbox Function
Hypothesis: Checkbox is not labeled and mimics a key action
Remedy: Add label (“Done?”) or replace with “Mark as done” button
Issue 3: Hidden Key Actions
Hypothesis: Important actions are buried in menus or expansions
Remedy: Surface actions like “Share to friend”” and “Share to Community” as visible buttons or add a share icon
Issue 4: Poor Navigation Context
Hypothesis: Back navigation lacks hierarchy and context
Remedy: Add labels (e.g., “← This week”) or home icon
Summary:

While the interface is generally easy to use, the main challenges come from unclear navigation, hidden actions, and lack of guidance. Improving visibility and clarity of key actions will significantly enhance the user experience.
